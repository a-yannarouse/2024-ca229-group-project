    Core Workout Routine

    Warm-up:

    5-10 minutes of light cardio (jumping jacks, jogging in place, or brisk walking)

    Core Workout Routine:

    • Plank: Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute

      • To perform a basic forearm plank, start by lying face down on the floor or a yoga mat, placing your forearms on the ground with elbows aligned below your shoulders and arms parallel to each other, about shoulder-width apart. Engage your core by pulling your belly button towards your spine, then push off the floor, raising up onto your toes and resting on your forearms, ensuring your body forms a straight line from head to heels without letting your hips sag or pike up, and maintain a neutral gaze.

      A plank primarily targets the core muscles, which include the rectus abdominis (front abs), transverse abdominis (deep core muscles), and the internal and external obliques (side of the torso). In addition to the core, planks also engage several other muscle groups throughout the body, making it a comprehensive exercise. Best for people who want toned and lean stomachs.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Russian Twists: 20 reps (10 twists on each side)

      • To perform a Russian twist, sit on the floor with your knees bent and feet flat, leaning back slightly to create a V-shape with your torso and thighs. Hold your hands together in front of your chest, or for added intensity, hold a weight. Engage your core and lift your feet slightly off the ground, if possible, to balance on your sit bones. Twist your torso to the right, bringing your hands or the weight towards the floor beside you, then twist to the left, engaging your abdominal and oblique muscles with each rotation. Keep your movements controlled and steady, and continue alternating sides for the desired number of repetitions.

      Russian twists primarily target the abdominal muscles, focusing on the obliques, which are the muscles on the sides of your abdomen, but they also engage the rectus abdominis (the front part of your abs) and the transverse abdominis (the deepest layer of abdominal muscles). Additionally, this exercise works the lower back, shoulders, and hip flexors, providing a comprehensive workout that improves core strength and rotational mobility. Best for people who want an hourglass body shape.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Leg Raises: 15 reps

      • To perform a leg raise, lie flat on your back on the floor or a mat with your legs straight out in front of you and your arms at your sides, palms down for stability. Engage your core by pressing your lower back into the floor, then slowly lift your legs off the ground to a 90-degree angle, keeping them straight and together. Pause briefly at the top, then slowly lower your legs back down without letting them touch the floor for the full set, maintaining control and keeping your abdominal muscles engaged throughout the movement.

      Leg raises primarily target the lower abdominal muscles, specifically the rectus abdominis, while also engaging the hip flexors and, to a lesser extent, the obliques, offering a focused workout that strengthens and tones the lower core, improves core stability, and enhances overall abdominal muscle definition. Best for people who want lean and muscular abs and stomach.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Bicycle Crunches: 20 reps (10 crunches on each side)

      • To perform bicycle crunches, lie on your back with your knees bent and feet lifted off the ground, placing your hands behind your head without interlocking your fingers. Engage your core and lift your shoulder blades off the ground, then twist your body to bring your right elbow towards your left knee while straightening your right leg. Switch sides by bringing your left elbow towards your right knee while simultaneously straightening your left leg, mimicking a pedaling motion. Continue alternating sides in a fluid, controlled manner, ensuring you fully extend each leg and maintain a steady pace to effectively engage your abdominal and oblique muscles.

      Bicycle crunches target the rectus abdominis, the main abdominal muscle, and are especially effective at engaging the obliques, the muscles on the sides of the abdomen, providing a comprehensive core workout that also involves the hip flexors and promotes improved balance, coordination, and flexibility. Best for people who want an hourglass body look.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Mountain Climbers: 30 seconds

      • To perform mountain climbers, start in a high plank position with your hands placed directly under your shoulders, your body in a straight line from head to heels, and your feet together. Engage your core, then quickly draw your right knee towards your chest without lifting your hips too high. Quickly switch legs, extending your right leg back to the starting position while simultaneously drawing your left knee towards your chest. Continue alternating legs at a rapid pace, maintaining a strong plank position with your back flat and hips level throughout the exercise to effectively engage your core, shoulders, and legs.

      Mountain climbers target multiple muscle groups, including the core muscles for stabilization, specifically engaging the rectus abdominis and obliques, while also working the deltoids, chest, and triceps for upper body strength, the quads in the legs, and the hamstrings for dynamic movement, making it an effective full-body workout that improves cardiovascular fitness, agility, and muscular endurance. Best for people who want a lean body figure.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Side Plank: Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute on each side

      • To perform a side plank, start by lying on your side with your legs straight and feet stacked. Place your elbow directly under your shoulder to prop up your upper body, with your forearm perpendicular to your body on the ground for stability. Engage your core and lift your hips off the ground, forming a straight line from your head to your feet. Keep your other hand on your hip or extend it towards the ceiling for balance. Hold this position, keeping your hips elevated and your body in a straight line, then switch sides and repeat the exercise to ensure balanced strength and muscle engagement on both sides of your body.

      Side planks primarily target the oblique muscles on the sides of the abdomen, offering a focused workout that strengthens and tones these areas, but they also engage the entire core including the rectus abdominis and transverse abdominis, as well as the shoulders, glutes, and hips, promoting improved core stability, balance, and posture. Best for people who want an hourglass and lean figure.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Dead Bug: 15 reps (alternating sides)

      • To perform the dead bug exercise, start by lying flat on your back on the floor or a mat, with your arms extended straight up towards the ceiling directly above your shoulders, and your legs raised with your knees bent at a 90-degree angle above your hips. Engage your core by pressing your lower back into the floor to eliminate any gap. Simultaneously lower your right arm behind your head and extend your left leg straight out, just above the floor, ensuring your lower back remains in contact with the floor throughout the movement. Return to the starting position and repeat the movement with the opposite arm and leg, alternating sides in a controlled manner to maintain core engagement and coordination throughout the exercise.

      The dead bug exercise targets the core muscles, specifically focusing on the rectus abdominis, transverse abdominis, and the obliques, enhancing core stability, coordination, and strength, while also engaging the hip flexors and providing a gentle workout for the arms and legs, making it an effective exercise for improving overall core function and reducing the risk of back pain.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Superman Exercise: 15 reps

      • To perform the Superman exercise, start by lying face down on the floor or a mat with your arms extended in front of you and your legs straight out behind you. Engage your core and glutes, then simultaneously lift your arms, chest, and legs off the floor as high as comfortably possible, keeping your neck neutral and gaze down to avoid straining. Hold this lifted position for a few seconds, feeling the engagement in your lower back, glutes, and upper back, then slowly lower back down to the starting position. Repeat the movement for the desired number of repetitions, ensuring to keep the movements controlled and deliberate to maximize the engagement of the targeted muscle groups.

      The Superman exercise targets the muscles in the lower back, specifically the erector spinae, along with engaging the glutes, hamstrings, shoulders, and upper back muscles, providing a comprehensive workout that strengthens the back, improves posture, and enhances spinal stability, making it beneficial for overall back health and function.

    Lower Body Workout Routine

    Warm-up:

    Do 5-10 minutes of light cardio (e.g., jogging, jumping jacks, cycling). In order to prevent future discomfort, do dynamic stretches like leg swings, hip circles, walking lunges.

    Routine:

    • Squats: 3 sets of 10-12 reps

      • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out. Keep your back straight and chest up. Lower your body by bending your knees and pushing your hips back, as if sitting into a chair. Keep your knees aligned with your toes. Push through your heels to return to the starting position.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Glute Bridge: 3 sets of 12-15 reps

      • Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart. Engage your core and squeeze your glutes as you lift your hips towards the ceiling, forming a straight line from shoulders to knees. Hold for a moment at the top, then slowly lower back down.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Lunges: 3 sets of 10 reps per leg

      • Start standing with feet together. Take a step forward with your right foot, lowering your body until both knees are bent at a 90-degree angle. Ensure your front knee is aligned with your ankle, and your back knee hovers just above the ground. Push back up to the starting position and repeat on the other side.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Calf Raises: 3 sets of 15-20 reps

      • Stand with feet hip-width apart, hands on hips or holding onto a stable surface for balance. Rise up onto the balls of your feet, lifting your heels as high as possible. Hold for a moment at the top, then lower back down.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Hip Thrusts: 3 sets of 10-12 reps

      • Sit on the floor with your upper back against a bench or elevated surface. Bend your knees and place your feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart. Rest a barbell or a weight plate across your hips. Drive through your heels, lifting your hips towards the ceiling until your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees. Squeeze your glutes at the top, then lower back down.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Reverse Lunges: 15 each arm, 3 reps (use whatever weight you are comfortable with)

      • Stand tall with feet hip-width apart. Step backward with your right foot, lowering your body until both knees are bent at a 90-degree angle. Your front knee should be aligned with your ankle, and your back knee should hover just above the ground. Push off your right foot to return to the starting position. Repeat on the other side.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    Cool Down:

    5-10 minutes of static stretching, focusing on the lower body muscles (Child Poses, Leg Swings, Biceps Stretch)

  • Child's Pose: The Child's Pose is a restorative yoga posture where you kneel on the floor with your toes together, knees hip-width apart, then sit back on your heels and fold forward, extending your arms out in front of you on the floor. This pose gently stretches the hips, thighs, and ankles while relaxing the muscles on the spine, shoulders, and neck, providing a sense of relaxation and stress relief, making it an ideal pose for resting and rejuvenating the body and mind.

    • Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

  • Leg Swings: Stand tall and hold onto a stable surface for balance. Swing one leg forward and backward in a controlled manner, keeping the leg straight. This helps loosen up the hip flexors and hamstrings.

  • Quad Stretch: Stand tall and grab one foot behind you, bringing it towards your glutes. Keep your knees close together and push your hips slightly forward to deepen the stretch. You should feel this in the front of your thigh (quadriceps).

    Upper Body Workout Routine

    Warm-up:

    5-10 minutes of light warm-up arm exercises (windmills, stretch out the arms, alternate toe touching, pushups)

    Upper Body Workout Routine:

    • Bench Press: Do 10 both arms, 3 reps (use whatever weight you are comfortable with)

      • To perform a bench press, lie flat on a bench, grip the barbell with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, lower it to your chest level, and then press it upwards until your arms are fully extended, ensuring to maintain a controlled motion throughout the exercise.

      The bench press primarily targets the pectoral muscles, while also engaging the triceps, shoulders (anterior deltoids), and to a lesser extent, the back muscles for stabilization. Best for people who want to gain muscle and have lean arms.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Overhead Press: Do 15 both arms, 3 reps (use whatever weight you feel comfortable with)

      • To perform an overhead press, also known as a military press, start by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart for stability. Grip a barbell or dumbbells with your hands just outside your shoulders, palms facing forward. Engage your core and slightly tuck your chin to create a straight line from your head to your pelvis. Press the weight upward in a controlled motion until your arms are fully extended above your head without locking your elbows. Keep your wrists strong and straight. Lower the weight back down to the starting position at shoulder level, ensuring the descent is controlled and the back remains neutral, avoiding excessive arching. It's crucial to maintain proper form to prevent injury and effectively target the intended musclesâ€”primarily the deltoids, triceps, and to a lesser extent, the upper chest and core for stabilization. As with any exercise, the techniques and understanding of biomechanics evolve, so staying informed about best practices is recommended for optimal performance and safety.

      The overhead press primarily targets the deltoid muscles in the shoulders, while also engaging the triceps, upper chest (pectoralis major), and core muscles for stabilization throughout the movement. Best for people who want to gain muscle and have lean arms.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Bicep Curls: 15 each arm, 3 reps (use whatever weight you are comfortable with)

      • To perform a bicep curl, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand with palms facing forward and arms extended downwards. Keep your elbows close to your torso, then curl the weights upward towards your shoulders by contracting your biceps. Make sure to keep your upper arms stationary, moving only your forearms. Pause briefly at the top of the curl, then slowly lower the dumbbells back to the starting position in a controlled manner, fully extending your arms.

      A bicep curl primarily targets the biceps brachii muscles in the upper arm, while also engaging the brachialis and brachioradialis muscles in the forearm to a lesser extent, effectively promoting arm strength and muscle growth. Best for people who want to gain muscle and have lean arms.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Dips: 20 reps

      • To perform dips, start by gripping parallel bars with your hands, arms straight, and shoulders above your hands. Lower your body by bending your elbows until your upper arms are parallel to the ground, keeping your elbows close to your body. Push back up to the starting position, extending your arms fully while maintaining a slight lean forward throughout the movement to engage your chest and triceps effectively.

      Dips primarily target the triceps, pectoral muscles, and the anterior deltoids in the shoulders, effectively building upper body strength and muscle definition. Best for people who want to loose arm fat.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Lateral Raises: 15 each arm, 3 reps (use whatever weight you are comfortable with)

      • To perform lateral raises, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand at your sides with palms facing in towards your body. Keep a slight bend in your elbows to protect your joints. Engage your core and lift the dumbbells out to the sides in a controlled manner, keeping your hands slightly tilted forward as if pouring water from a jug, until your arms are parallel to the floor. Pause at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the weights back to the starting position, maintaining control throughout the descent to effectively target the shoulder muscles.

      Lateral raises primarily target the deltoid muscles in the shoulders, specifically the lateral or middle part, helping to enhance shoulder width and definition, while also engaging the upper traps and the supraspinatus, one of the rotator cuff muscles, for stabilization. Best for people who want to loose arm fat.

      Below is a youtube video showcasing the workout:

    • Dumbbell Clean: 15 each arm, 3 reps (use whatever weight you are comfortable with)

      • To perform a dumbbell clean, begin by standing with your feet hip-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand in front of your thighs with palms facing your body. Bend at your hips and knees to lower into a squat position, keeping the dumbbells close to your body. Explosively extend your hips, knees, and ankles, simultaneously shrugging your shoulders to lift the dumbbells. As the dumbbells ascend, quickly rotate your wrists to catch the dumbbells at shoulder height in a front squat position, with your elbows pointing forward. Stand up straight to complete the movement, then lower the dumbbells back to the starting position in a controlled manner, preparing for the next repetition. This exercise combines elements of a squat, an explosive pull, and a catch, effectively targeting the lower body, core, shoulders, and arms.

      The dumbbell clean primarily targets the lower body muscles, including the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes, as well as the upper body, specifically the deltoids, trapezius, and forearms, while also engaging the core muscles for stabilization throughout the explosive movement, offering a comprehensive full-body workout. Best for people who want lean and muscular arms.

However, if we interpret this as performing a pull-up with an added motion to simulate a "clean," a plausible description might be: Begin by hanging from a pull-up bar with an overhand grip, hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Pull yourself up explosively, aiming to bring your chest to the bar while keeping your body straight and core engaged. At the top of the movement, rather than simply lowering back down, you could add a motion to mimic the "clean" by quickly pulling your knees up towards your chest in a rapid, clean-like motion before extending your legs again as you lower yourself to the starting position in a controlled manner. This additional knee raise at the top could engage the core and mimic the explosive component of a clean. Remember, since this is not a standard exercise, it's crucial to focus on form and safety to prevent injury.

      A pull-up primarily targets the latissimus dorsi muscles in the back, as well as the biceps, trapezius, and forearm muscles, making it an effective exercise for developing upper body strength and muscle definition. Best for people who want lean and muscular arms.

    • Skull Crusher: 15 each arm, 3 reps (use whatever weight you are comfortable with)

      • To perform a skull crusher, lie on your back on a bench with your feet flat on the ground. Hold a barbell or EZ curl bar with a close grip (hands about shoulder-width apart) directly above your chest, arms fully extended. Keeping your upper arms stationary, bend your elbows to slowly lower the weight towards your forehead. Stop just before the weight touches your forehead, then extend your arms back to the starting position by contracting your triceps. Ensure the movement is controlled, focusing on moving through the triceps without moving your upper arms to maximize effectiveness and minimize risk of injury.

      A skull crusher primarily targets the triceps muscles at the back of the upper arm, effectively isolating and strengthening this area for improved arm definition and strength. Best for people who want lean and muscular arms.
